=== 5. Experiment Findings (Summary) ===

  The experiment demonstrates that EvidenceCollection.aggregate() does
  blend heterogeneous condition signals:

    Condition                   Avg Return    Confidence    Decision
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    cpi_pressure=down           +0.9122%     0.5990         POSITIVE
    cpi_pressure=up             +0.4328%     0.5488         POSITIVE
    Merged (baseline)           +0.6725%     0.5739         POSITIVE

  Magnitude compression:
    - "down" signal is 2.1x stronger than merged implies
    - "up" signal is 36% weaker than merged implies
  Confidence compression:
    - "down" confidence is 0.025 higher than merged
    - "up" confidence is 0.025 lower than merged

  No directional flip occurred because both conditions are positive.
  However, the magnitude and confidence signals are materially
  compressed by aggregate().

  With more divergent conditions (e.g., cpi_pressure=down vs up with
  US10Y trend), aggregate() could produce a neutral decision while
  both per-condition decisions are directional — a complete signal
  cancellation scenario.

=== 6. Recommendations ===

  1. EXP-002 is the evidence base for any future aggregate() redesign.
     Current findings confirm signal compression is real and measurable.

  2. For multi-condition scenarios (e.g., CPI+US10Y with 4 condition
     combinations × 3 horizons = 12 evidence items), the signal
     cancellation is likely worse. Run EXP-002 with
     condition_columns=("cpi_pressure", "us10y_trend") to quantify.

  3. No action required until the experiment results demonstrate a
     statistically significant signal loss that impacts decision quality.
